About Me

Hi! I'm Jeff. I'm just a dude living in San Francisco, CA that likes to make software for good, hike and bike everywhere, and teach about what I know and do via public speaking (I won't go into it, but I'm a Toastmaster).

The last ten years have been a fun adventure! I lived in the wonderful city of Rochester, NY where I studied Computer Science, Journalism, and Applied Communication at RIT. While there, I also first discovered my love of the outdoors, eventually going on to lead the school's Outing Club.

As the Rochester chapter of my life closed, I moved to Philadelphia (a super awesome city!). I started off as a software developer at Vanguard and had the opportunity to rotate across many jobs and experience nearly every part of the software developer lifecycle (SDLC): configuration management, user interface development, software testing, research and development, backend development, tool adoption, and training (developer evangelist style). This was part of the Technology Leadership Program, a sort of acceleration into management program.

Vanguard was a great place to start and is a wonderful company, but financial services weren't my passion and I set out for something new. I did a quick bounce at MEDecision before landing at Johnson & Johnson's Lifescan as a Senior Software Developer in Research & Development. I mostly worked on OneTouch Reveal (an online diabetes logbook and pattern detector) as a backend Java developer, but also got to lead tech talks, develop prototypes, and visit diabetes clinics in Europe.

As if I didn't geek out enough at work, I have a passion for volunteering my tech skills. After attending a Code for Philly event, I wound up volunteering to make a prototype vacant land tracker with the Philadelphia Association of Community Development Corporations. The prototype came to be known as Reclaim Philly.

Through Reclaim Philly and some other volunteer projects, I became much more aware of the civic tech community and a non-profit called Code for America (CfA) that is at the epicenter of the civic tech movement. Leaping at the opportunity to use my tech skills to improve our most important institutions (government), I applied for and was selected for CfA's one year Fellowship program starting in January 2014. Even though I hopped coasts to be in the program in San Francisco, CA; my partner for the year is the State of Rhode Island, where I'm helping to create open source applications for education systems. It's been an incredible experience where I've done everything from user-centered design to community organizing to project management (on top of the obvious coding).

With the CfA Fellowship ending really soon (end of November 2014), I regularly get asked, "what's next?"

Over the last few years, I've really taken a liking to working for mission-driven organizations that use technology make the world a better/happier place (examples: J&J has the Credo and CfA helps governments created services more in touch with the needs of its community) and would love for my next gig to be at such a government/company/organization. While I love San Francisco and might stay, I'm not cemented here and am open to jobs in other cool cities (especially those that have lively 20s/30s scenes and that are near hiking/biking trails).
