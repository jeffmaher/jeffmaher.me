About Me

Hi! I'm Jeff. I'm just a dude living in San Francisco, CA that likes to make software for good, hike and bike everywhere, and teach about what I know and do via public speaking.

I'm currently a Fellow with Code for America, working with the State of Rhode Island to make open source school registration applications. I absolutely love working there, but it's only a year of service and I'll be looking to start a new job for January 2015.

Through my last two jobs, I've come to appreciate organizations that are mission-driven and use technology to make the world a better/happier place (examples: J&J has the Credo and CfA helps governments become more in touch with the needs of their communities). I'd love for my next gig to be with such an organization/government/company.

While I'm currently in San Francisco, I'm open to other cities that have interesting work, good 20s/30s scenes, decent public transit, and hiking/biking trails nearby.

Recent Jobs

Code for America (San Francisco)

2014 Fellow

Johnson & Johnson - Lifescan (Philadelphia)

Senior Software Developer in R&D
  • Worked on an online diabetes management platform called OneTouch Reveal. It allows patterns to be recognized in patients' blood glucose levels (based on uploaded data from insulin pumps and blood glucose meters).
  • Optimized data transfers between medical devices and OneTouch Reveal by making it 15 times faster and providing a REST/JSON API
  • Started and spoke at regular tech talks, designed to teach new skills and introduce new ideas for R&D possibilities

MEDecision (Philadelphia)

Senior Software Developer
  • Vastly improved developer productivity by leading an effort to switch revision control systems from ClearCase to Subversion. Average daily time spent on revision control tasks dropped from 1+ hours to 5-15 minutes.
  • Implemented a new event-oriented architecture auditing designed to capture user changes to patient data
  • Aided team with improving communication with off-shore developers

Vanguard (Philadelphia)

Software Developer - Technology Leadership Program
  • Rotated departments every eight months as part of the Technology Leadership Program. This provided indepth exposure to all sides of the software development lifecycle.
  • Supported better developer coding practices by providing training and better tooling/frameworks (examples: using continuous integration, making design more modular with dependency injection frameworks, and utilizing dependency management tools)
  • Brought in a rules engine to make business rule implementation easier for Vanguard's order management system
